Killavic wants short responses with a recommendation, not a fork-in-the-road menu. When they say "im not sure", that means **make the call** and tell them what you picked — don't re-list the options. They've also explicitly enabled auto mode, signalling "just do things."

**Why:** Through this session, every time I gave 2 options and asked them to pick, the friction showed (e.g. "im not sure" when I split eject UX into two designs). When I picked one and explained the tradeoff in passing, work moved.

**How to apply:** Default to a single concrete recommendation with a one-sentence "here's why." Save options-lists for genuinely high-stakes / irreversible decisions. After completing work that has a small obvious follow-up (e.g. "also update memory"), just do it without asking. They don't need updates on routine internals — focus user-facing text on what changed and what to test.
